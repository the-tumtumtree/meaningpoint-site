We were once asked to develop a name for a digital tool designed to support people throughout GLP-1 therapy. It helps users track changes across their entire body while providing guidance, advice, and reassurance along the way.

The result was Glapp — a combination of GLP and app. The name also sounds like a simple action verb, similar to tap, slap, or clap. Since the therapy involves regular shots, we wanted the app’s name to suggest a friendlier and more positive counterpart to that recurring action.

It was one of eight short, punchy options, but the team took to it immediately — and the name has since become a firm favourite with both the team and its users around the world.
