One of the AI companion’s goals was to inspire users. So every day, a new short story had to appear in the app — 4–5 screens on a useful or educational topic. Nutrition, vitamins, cognitive biases, hobbies, diagnostic methods, and so on. As usual, our resources were limited. So from the very beginning, we decided to use AI.

At first, the texts generated by neural networks, even with the most detailed prompts and outlines, always felt a little too AI-generated: too many empty phrases, repetitions, and bits that sounded meaningful without actually saying much. So we decided to work with examples instead. We came up with a few simple content categories and wrote a reference text for each one: what avocados contain, why knitting is good for you, or what the fundamental attribution error is.

The results were amazing. The whole process started taking 5–6 hours per month instead of 20. The quality turned out to be more than good enough, and the share of story views didn't drop — in fact, it grew by 4–5%.

Still, for some reason, we never felt ready to step out of the process completely and leave everything to the LLM. Apparently, our own biases are still pretty strong 🙄
